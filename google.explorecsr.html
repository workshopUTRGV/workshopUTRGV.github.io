Welcome!

The University of Texas RGV Computer Science is proud to partner with Google Research to offer exploreCSR for a rich research experience for undergraduate students. We encourage students from historically-underrepresented groups to apply, including but not limited to -- Hispanic, women, students of color, first-generation college students, students from low socioeconomic backgrounds, and LGBTQ+ students.

Our goal is to expose students to all aspects of computing research from faculty from different universities, gain their confidence to pursue graduate studies, develop practical skills, and achieve a sense of belong in computing research.


This workshop covers five research areas:

(1). data mining and artificial intelligence;

(2). pervasive computing and healthcare;

(3). distributed databases and its applications;

(4). computational mathematics;

(5). robotics and assistive technology.

About the Program

Who can apply: undergraduate students (including graduating seniors). The program in this year is virtual due to the COVID-19 pandemic and in the US Central time zone.

Experience needed: no prior research experience necessary. Coursework in introduction of computer science, calculus, linear algebra, and probability are helpful.

Research activities: 1). workshop participants will be paired with a faculty mentor throughout the two-day event. 2). internship participants will be paired with one or two faculty mentors through a eight-week research project.

Multiple projects are available, inclusing literature reviews, replications of prior work, improvements of existing research projects, or new independent research projects. The internship participants will present their work in posters at the end of July, 2021 and may extend to write and publish those into short papers.

Time commitment: The workshop is two day events and 12 faculty will present and engage with students. The summer research internship is a eight-week long expereince. We expect research group meetings, mentor meetings, and project work to take a minimum of 4 hours per day.

Stipend: to offset the opportunity cost while attending the workshop, each workshop participant will receive a $50 stipend. Each internship participant will receive $2,000 in total (4 hours a day with $12.5 per hour).

FAQ
  • I am an undergraduate student in my final year; I will graduate in 2021. Am I eligible to apply?
    Yes.
  • I am an undergraduate student from [any university or college institution, any country]. Am I eligible to apply?
    Yes. Please note that UTRGV is on Central Time, many of our events will be in those working hours.
  • Is this program only for underrepresented groups?
    The goal of the exploreCSR program is to support underrepresented students to pursue computing research (the program). Though we consider all applicants, we focus on this goal.
  • I'm a high school student. Am I eligible to apply?
    Not this time.
  • Are the applications first come, first serve?
    No. We will consider all applications received by the deadline (May 1st, 2021).
  • How much details should I put into the questions on the form, e.g., research interests?
    Short one paragraph is good. We would like to match you to the right people and projects.
  • I didn't receive a confirmation of my application.
    It should go through. If you're worried, please submit another one.
  • I submitted my form, but received the error. What should I do?
    You should be able to get around this by filling out the form while logged into to a personal Google account.
