exploreCSR 2020-2021: Computing Research

Virtual Workshop Date: May 28th-29th, 2021.
Virtual Research Internship Date: June 7th - July 30th ( 8 weeks ), 2021.

Note: Internships are limited and will be competitive, please apply early! Internships are matched based on the research interests filled out in the application form.

Welcome

The University of Texas RGV Computer Science is proud to partner with Google Research to offer exploreCSR for a rich research experience for undergraduate students. We encourage students from historically-underrepresented groups to apply, including but not limited to -- Hispanic, women, students of color, first-generation college students, students from low socioeconomic backgrounds, and LGBTQ+ students.

Our goal is to expose students to all aspects of computing research from faculty from different universities, gain their confidence to pursue graduate studies, develop practical skills, and achieve a sense of belonging in computing research.

This workshop covers five research areas:

  1. data mining and artificial intelligence
  2. pervasive computing and healthcare
  3. distributed databases and its applications
  4. computational mathematics
  5. robotics and assistive technology

About

Who can apply
undergraduate students (including graduating seniors). The program in this year is virtual due to the COVID-19 pandemic and in the US Central time zone.
Experience needed
no prior research experience necessary. Coursework in introduction of computer science, calculus, linear algebra, and probability are helpful.
Research activities
(1) workshop participants will be paired with a faculty mentor throughout the two-day event
(2) internship participants will be paired with one or two faculty mentors through a eight-week research project.

Multiple projects are available, including literature reviews, replications of prior work, improvements of existing research projects, or new independent research projects. The internship participants will present their work in posters at the end of July, 2021 and may extend to write and publish those into short papers.

Time commitment
the workshop is two day events and 12 faculty will present and engage with students. The summer research internship is a eight-week long expereince. We expect research group meetings, mentor meetings, and project work to take a minimum of 4 hours per day.
Stipend
to offset the opportunity cost while attending the workshop, each workshop participant will receive a $50 stipend. Each internship participant will receive $2,000 in total (4 hours a day with $12.5 per hour).
